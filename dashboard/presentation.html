The Core Recruitment Strategy

Methodology

❌ The Keyword Trap

The job description specifies a trap built in the dataset: "The right answer is not 'find candidates whose skills section contains the most AI keywords'."

  • Candidates stuff resumes with hundreds of unverified skills.
  • Keyword-matching filters miss genuine founding-team engineering talent.
  • A recruiter needs to check experience depth and verify production skills.

✔️ Our Multi-Signal Approach

We rank profiles using a holistic scoring formula consisting of:

  • Semantic Analysis (40%): Latent conceptual context from JDs and profiles.
  • Structural Validation (40%): Experience tenure, titles, skill duration.
  • Behavioral Indicators (20%): Platform recency, notice periods, and code quality.
  • Honeypot Shield: Heavy penalty filters for suspicious and contradictory profiles.

Two-Phase Architecture

Architecture

The challenge requires processing 100,000 candidates in under 5 minutes on a standard CPU. We resolved this constraint with a high-performance two-phase architecture.

1

Text Aggregation

Aggregates candidates' summaries, experience, and duration-filtered skills into a narrative document.

2

Pre-Computation

Applies TF-IDF vectorization (30K features) and Truncated SVD (384 dimensions) to cache candidates' dense vector embeddings.

3

JD Matching

Computes cosine similarity between the Job Description embedding and the 100K pre-computed candidate vectors (completed in milliseconds).

4

Signal Fusion

Applies structural constraints, behavioral scores, honeypot filters, and penalties to compile the final top 100 CSV.

Signal Fusion & Scoring System

Scoring

📊 Scoring Weights

  • Semantic Score (40%): Cosine similarity between JD vector and Candidate LSA vector. Finds conceptual match.
  • Structural Score (40%): Title match (Senior, Founding, Lead), experience target (6-8 years ideal), and production-grade skills (e.g. AWS, MLOps, vector databases).
  • Behavioral Score (20%): Active Open-to-Work flags, high recruiter response rate, shorter notice periods, and GitHub activity index.

⚠️ Penalties & Hard Filters

  • Honeypot Filter (*0.01 multiplier): Candidates listing 10+ AI skills but having 0 months of career experience.
  • Consulting Penalty (*0.10 multiplier): Outlines candidates who spent their entire careers in service-only IT agencies.
  • Experience/Location Bounds: Adjusts scores for candidates below 2 years of experience or residing outside the target region.

Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) Deep Dive

Natural Language Processing

💡 Why TF-IDF + SVD?

Python 3.14 on Windows has compatibility issues with PyTorch and ONNX runtimes.

  • LSA (TF-IDF combined with Truncated SVD) has zero heavy native DLL dependencies and runs flawlessly on Python 3.14.
  • Builds a dense **384-dimensional** semantic space of candidates.
  • Understands synonym relationships (e.g., matching "vector search" to "FAISS" or "semantic search").
# Dense cosine similarity math
candidate_embeddings = np.load('candidate_embeddings.npy')
jd_vector = tfidf.transform([jd_text])
jd_embedding = svd.transform(jd_vector)
similarities = cosine_similarity(jd_embedding, candidate_embeddings)
